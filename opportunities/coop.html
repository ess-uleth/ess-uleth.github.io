What is Co-op?

Co-operative education allows students to gain paid, hands-on work experience related to their field of study while completing their degree. For English students, co-op can be a valuable way to explore careers connected to communication, publishing, education, administration, editing, research, public relations, nonprofit work, and more.

Why consider it?

Co-op gives students the chance to build professional experience before graduation. It can help strengthen a resume, develop workplace confidence, and make connections with employers and community organizations. It is especially useful for students who want to understand how skills from an English degree transfer into professional settings.

Skills you can build

  • Professional writing and editing
  • Communication and public-facing work
  • Research and information management
  • Project coordination
  • Workplace collaboration

Who should look into this?

Co-op may be a good fit for students who want paid experience, are curious about career pathways after an English degree, or want to connect their academic work to practical professional settings.

Students interested in co-op should consult the University of Lethbridge co-operative education office or academic advising to confirm eligibility, deadlines, and application requirements.
