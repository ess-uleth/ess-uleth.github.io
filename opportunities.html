Get the most out of your degree

Opportunities For You

You deserve to make the most of your degree! The ESS has collected a list of all the opportunities available to students interested in English and all related disciplines at the University of Lethbridge.

Jobs, Volunteer, and Experience

Co-op

Co-operative education (co-op) allows students to gain paid, hands-on work experience related to their field of study while completing their degree. Through co-op placements, English students can explore careers in areas such as communications, publishing, education, and administration. These experiences help build professional skills, strengthen resumes, and provide valuable connections before graduation.

Applied Studies

Applied Studies courses give students the opportunity to earn academic credit while gaining practical work experience. Students can pursue placements in workplaces related to their interests, allowing them to apply classroom knowledge in real-world settings. This is a flexible option for students looking to explore potential career paths while continuing their studies.

The ESS

Serving as an executive member of the ESS is a great way to gain leadership and organizational experience. Executives work collaboratively to plan events, communicate with students, and represent the interests of the English student community. This role helps develop skills in teamwork, project management, and professional communication, while also making a meaningful impact on the student experience.

The Whetstone

Whetstone is a biannual literary magazine managed by student-enthusiasts at the University of Lethbridge with occasional help from members of the English Department. Originally established in 1971, Whetstone was revived in September 2009 and aims to attract writers from southern Alberta and the prairies. Getting involved with the Whetstone is a great opportunity for students passionate about writing, editing, and publishing.

The Meliorist

The Meliorist is an independent, student-focused nonprofit based at the University of Lethbridge. Since 1967, it has supported emerging storytellers, amplified diverse voices, and connected communities through creative and dynamic publishing. Students can get involved as writers, editors, or members of the executive team, gaining valuable experience in publishing, leadership, and collaborative creative work. It is an excellent opportunity for those interested in storytelling, editing, and being part of a long-standing campus publication.

Advanced Research

Independent Study

Independent Studies courses allow students to pursue a self-directed research project under the supervision of a faculty member. This opportunity is ideal for exploring a specific topic in greater depth, whether in literature, writing, or language. Students develop skills in research, critical thinking, and academic writing while working closely with a supervisor to design and complete a project tailored to their interests.

Honours' Thesis

An Honours Thesis is a major independent research project available to students in their fourth year or higher with a GPA of 3.5 or above. Students work under the supervision of a faculty member to develop a research question, conduct in-depth analysis, and produce a substantial piece of scholarly writing. This experience builds advanced research and writing skills and is especially valuable for students considering graduate studies.

Conferences

Academic conferences provide students with the opportunity to present their research, share ideas, and engage with a wider scholarly community. Whether presenting a paper or a poster, conferences help students build confidence, develop communication skills, and gain experience in academic and professional settings. They are also a valuable way to network, receive feedback on your work, and explore potential future pathways in research and graduate studies.

Competitions and Funding

The Bridge Prize

The Bridge Prize is a national short story competition presented by the School of Liberal Education at the University of Lethbridge. Established in 2020, it is a biennial competition that celebrates excellence in student fiction writing and encourages the next generation of Canadian writers. Open to undergraduate and graduate students across Canada, the prize offers an exciting opportunity to share original work, gain recognition, and become part of a wider literary community.

Chinook Summer Research Award

The Chinook Summer Research Award is a competitive undergraduate research award at the University of Lethbridge that supports students in conducting full-time research over the summer under the supervision of a faculty member. Open to students across disciplines, the award provides funding to allow students to focus on a research project of their own design or contribute to a larger faculty-led project. The Chinook is an excellent opportunity to gain hands-on research experience, build academic skills, and explore potential pathways into graduate studies.

George Ellis Research Scholarship

The George Ellis Research Scholarship is awarded to outstanding undergraduate students at the University of Lethbridge who are engaged in research in the humanities, social sciences, or fine arts. The scholarship recognizes academic excellence and supports students in pursuing independent or faculty-supervised research projects. It provides both financial support and recognition, making it a valuable opportunity for students looking to deepen their research experience and strengthen their academic profile.

Travel and Conference Funding

Travel and conference grants are available to help offset the costs of attending academic conferences. The Students' Union offers funding through an application process to support expenses such as travel and registration, though it does not typically cover the full cost. Additional funding may be available through organizations such as the Lethbridge Public Interest Research Group for conferences that align with their mission, and The Meliorist can assist with printing costs for conference materials such as posters.
